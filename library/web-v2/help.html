★ Getting Started

Welcome to The Library. Here you can browse, listen to, and download audiobooks from a curated private collection.

Browsing as a Guest

You can explore the entire library without an account — browse books, search, filter, and read descriptions. To listen to or download audiobooks, you'll need to sign in or request access.

First Time Here?

If you have an account, sign in using your chosen authentication method (see Authentication Methods below). If you don't have an account yet, click Request Access to ask the administrator for an invitation.

Quick Overview

📚

Browse

Scroll the grid of audiobook cards showing covers, titles, and authors.

🔍

Search

Find books by title, author, or narrator using the search bar.

🎧

Listen

Click any book card to start playing. Your position is saved automatically.

📥

Download

Download audiobooks for offline listening (if your account has download permission).

📚 Browsing the Library

The main library page shows your audiobook collection as a grid of cards.

Book Cards

Each card displays:

  • Cover art — the audiobook's cover image (or a placeholder if unavailable)
  • Title — the book's title in gold text
  • Author & Narrator — who wrote and who narrates the book
  • Duration — total listening time
  • Format — the audio format (Opus, M4B, MP3, etc.)

Stats Bar

At the top of the page, you'll see the library statistics: total volumes, hours of audio, number of authors, and number of narrators.

Editions

Some books have multiple editions (different narrators or formats). Look for the Editions badge on a card. Click it to expand and see all available versions, each with its own Play button.

Progress Indicator

If you've partially listened to a book, you'll see a progress bar on the card showing how far along you are. The Play button tooltip will show your saved position (e.g., "Resume from 12:34").

🔍 Search & Filter

Text Search

Type in the search bar to find books by title, author, or narrator. Results update as you type. Click Clear to reset your search.

Author Filter

Use the author dropdown to filter books by a specific author. You can:

  • Type to search for an author by name
  • Use the letter group buttons (A-E, F-J, etc.) to narrow the list
  • Click the A-Z / Z-A toggle to change sort order
  • Click the × button to clear the author filter

Narrator Filter

Works exactly like the author filter, but filters by narrator instead. Book counts are shown next to each narrator's name.

⇅ Sorting

Use the sort dropdown to change how books are ordered. Available options:

Category Options
Title A-Z or Z-A
Author By last name, first name, or full name (A-Z / Z-A)
Narrator By last name or first name (A-Z / Z-A)
Duration Longest first or Shortest first
Dates Recently / Oldest acquired; Newest / Oldest published
Grouped View Author (Grouped A-Z) or Narrator (Grouped A-Z) — collapsible headers
Series & Editions Series A-Z/Z-A (with sequence order); Edition A-Z

Grouped View

The Author (Grouped A-Z) and Narrator (Grouped A-Z) sort options display books organized under collapsible headers, sorted by last name. Click any header to expand or collapse that group. This is especially useful for exploring which books belong to the same author or narrator in large libraries.

📎 Collections

Collections are curated groups of books organized by category (genre, series, themes, etc.).

Using Collections

  • Click the Collections button (in the results bar) to open the sidebar
  • Click any collection name to filter the library to just those books
  • The active collection is shown as a badge next to the Collections button
  • Click Clear Filter at the bottom of the sidebar to show all books again
  • Click the × button or the overlay to close the sidebar

📖 My Library

The My Library tab shows your personal collection — books you've listened to, downloaded, or have in progress. Switch to it using the tab button in the navigation bar.

What's Included

  • Books in progress — any book where you have a saved listening position, with a progress bar showing how far you've gotten
  • Downloaded books — books you've downloaded for offline listening, with the date of your most recent download
  • Listening history — a record of which books you've listened to and when, sorted by most recent activity

Progress Bars

Each book card in My Library includes a visual progress bar indicating how much of the book you've completed. The percentage is calculated from your last saved playback position relative to the book's total duration.

Hiding & Unhiding Books

You can hide finished or unwanted books from your My Library view to keep it focused on what you're currently reading:

  • Each book card has a checkbox in the corner for selection
  • Select one or more books, then click the Hide Selected button to remove them from the grid
  • A Hidden (N) pill badge shows how many books you've hidden — click it to switch to the hidden books view
  • In the hidden view, select books and click Unhide Selected to restore them to your main My Library grid

Hiding a book is purely a view preference — all your data is preserved. Your listening positions, history, and download records remain intact and the book is still available in the full library.

✨ New Books

How It Works

  • Hover over the marquee to pause the scrolling and read at your own pace
  • Click the × button on the right side to dismiss the banner

🎧 Audio Player

The player appears at the bottom of the screen when you start playing a book.

Controls

  • Play / Pause — the center button toggles playback
  • Progress bar — drag to seek to any position
  • -30s / +30s — skip backward or forward 30 seconds
  • Speed — cycle through playback speeds: 0.5x, 0.75x, 1x, 1.25x, 1.5x, 1.75x, 2x, 2.5x
  • Volume slider — adjust playback volume
  • × — close the player

Streaming Only

The Library streams audio directly from its own server — it cannot access or play files stored on your device. This is by design for privacy and security. To listen offline, download the audiobook and use a local player app.

Player Info

The player shows the book's cover art, title, author, audiobook ID, and file path. Click the path to copy it.

🔖 Progress Tracking

Your listening progress is tracked per-user and saved automatically as you listen. You can close the browser, switch devices, or come back days later — the library remembers exactly where you were.

How It Works

  • Positions are saved every 5 seconds during playback, tied to your user account
  • When you return to a book, the Play button automatically resumes from your last position
  • Scrubbing, skipping forward (+30s), and skipping back (-30s) also save your position immediately
  • Positions sync across devices automatically — start on your laptop, pick up on your phone
  • Your progress appears in the My Library tab alongside your listening history

Resuming

Click the Play button on any book card (or within an edition's actions) to continue from your last position. If you haven't listened to a book before, Play starts from the beginning.

📥 Downloads

If your account has download permission, you'll see a green Download button on book cards.

  • Click the button to download the audiobook file to your device
  • Downloads are the original audio file (Opus, M4B, etc.)
  • If you don't see a Download button, your account may not have download permission — contact the administrator

Why Download?

The Library streams audiobooks directly from its own server storage — it cannot access files stored on your device. This is by design for privacy and security. If you'd like to listen offline or use a dedicated audiobook player with features like bookmarking, sleep timers, and variable speed, download the file and open it in one of the apps below.

Recommended Audiobook Players

Downloaded files are in Opus format (.opus), which is widely supported. Here are some good players for each platform:

  • Windows — VLC Media Player (free, open source) or foobar2000 (free, lightweight)
  • Mac — VLC Media Player (free, open source) or IINA (free, native macOS design)
  • Linux — VLC Media Player (free, open source) or Celluloid (free, GTK-based)
  • iOS — VLC for Mobile (free) or BookMobile (audiobook-focused)
  • Android — VLC for Android (free) or Smart AudioBook Player (audiobook-focused)

Download History

Your downloads are tracked per-user. Books you've downloaded appear in your My Library tab with the date of your most recent download. This helps you keep track of which books you already have for offline listening.

🔒 Authentication Methods

The Library supports three ways to sign in. Your administrator may set your initial method when they invite you, and you can change it later in your profile.

Authenticator App (TOTP)

Use an app like 2FAS, Google Authenticator, or Aegis to generate a 6-digit code that changes every 30 seconds. This is the most common method and works without internet access on your phone.

Passkey / Security Key

Use your device's biometric sensor (fingerprint, face recognition) or a physical security key (like a YubiKey). Passkeys are the most convenient — just tap to sign in. Your browser must support WebAuthn.

Email Link (Magic Link)

Receive a sign-in link via email. Click the link to be logged in automatically — no codes or passwords needed. Links expire after 15 minutes and can only be used once. This is the simplest method for non-technical users.

Changing Your Method

Open your account by clicking your avatar in the header bar, then use Switch Method under Authentication to choose a new method. You'll go through a brief setup process (scanning a QR code for TOTP, registering a passkey, or confirming your email for magic link). See Your Profile for details.

👤 Your Profile

When signed in, click your avatar/name button in the header bar to open My Account.

Profile

Click your username or email in the account panel to edit them inline. Your username initial appears on the avatar circle in the header.

Your current sign-in method is shown as a badge. Use Switch Method to change it (TOTP, Magic Link, or Passkey), or Reset Credentials to regenerate your current method's setup. See Authentication Methods for what each option means.

🔒 Stay Logged In

When you sign in, you can check Stay logged in on this device to keep your session active indefinitely — you won't need to sign in again until you explicitly sign out.

How It Works

  • A secure session key is stored in your browser so you're automatically recognized when you return
  • Your session stays active until you sign out — there is no inactivity timeout
  • Signing out (or clearing browser data) ends the session and requires a fresh sign-in

Privacy & Security

  • Anyone with access to this device could use your Library account while you're logged in
  • Only use this on a device you trust
  • To fully sign out, use the Sign Out button in your account panel — this clears your session from the device

Passkey Suggestion

If you sign in with email links and enable "stay logged in," you may see a one-time suggestion to set up a passkey instead. Passkeys provide even more reliable persistent login because they're tied to your device's biometric hardware.

❔ Frequently Asked Questions

Can I listen on multiple devices?

Yes. Your listening position syncs automatically across all devices where you're signed in. Start a book on your laptop and pick up right where you left off on your phone.

Why can't I see the Download button?

Downloads require permission from the administrator. If you don't see a Download button on book cards, your account may not have download access. Contact the admin through your profile menu to request it.

What audio format are the books in?

Most books are in Opus format (.opus), a modern audio codec with excellent quality at small file sizes. Opus is supported by VLC, most modern browsers, and many audiobook player apps. See the Downloads section for recommended players.

How do I find books by a specific author?

You have three options: use the Search bar to type the author's name, use the Author Filter dropdown to select from a categorized list, or choose Author (Grouped A-Z) from the sort menu to see all books organized under collapsible author headers.

What is the difference between sorting and grouped view?

Regular sorting (like "Author A-Z") arranges books in a flat list ordered by the author's name. Grouped view organizes books under collapsible headers — one per author or narrator — so you can see at a glance which books belong to each person and expand only the ones you're interested in.

Can I request new audiobooks?

Yes. When signed in, click your name in the header, then select Contact Admin to send a request for new titles.

What are Collections?

Collections are curated groups of books organized by genre, series, or theme (e.g., Fiction, Mystery & Thriller, Great Courses). Open the Collections sidebar to browse and filter by category.

How does "Stay Logged In" work?

When you check Stay logged in on this device during sign-in, a secure session key is stored in your browser. You'll stay signed in indefinitely until you explicitly sign out. See Stay Logged In for details and security considerations.

How do I resume a book I was listening to?

Just click the Play button on any book. If you've listened to it before, playback automatically resumes from your last saved position. The Play button tooltip shows exactly where you'll pick up (e.g., "Resume from 1:23:45").

Do media keys work with the player?

Yes. Play/Pause, Next Track (+30s), and Previous Track (-30s) media keys work during playback. On mobile, lockscreen controls also integrate with the player. See Keyboard & Media Keys.
